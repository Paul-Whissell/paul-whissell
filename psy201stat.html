Learning Objectives

By working through these 69 problems across Lectures 1–4, you should be able to:

  • Distinguish populations from samples and descriptive from inferential statistics, and classify variables by research design and scale of measurement.
  • Calculate and interpret measures of central tendency and variability — mean, median, mode, sum of squares, variance, and standard deviation — and convert between raw scores and z-scores.
  • Use the normal distribution and the distribution of sample means to find probabilities, compute standard error, and judge how likely a sample outcome is.
  • Conduct and interpret a complete hypothesis test: state hypotheses, set alpha and the critical region, compute z, decide, and report effect size (Cohen's d) and power.
